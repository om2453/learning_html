The BMW M1 (model code E26) was BMW’s first mid-engine production car and the only one until the i8. It was developed by BMW Motorsport GmbH under Jochen Neerpasch to compete in sports car racing and to rival Porsche in Group 5 racing

The standard BMW M2 is powered by a 3.0-liter M TwinPower Turbo inline-six engine producing 480 hp and 600 Nm of torque, paired with either a 6-speed manual or an 8-speed M Steptronic Sport transmission. It accelerates from 0–100 km/h in 4.0 seconds with the automatic transmission and 4.2 seconds with the manual, offering precise handling and rear-wheel-drive dynamics
