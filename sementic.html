This is the first BMW M car

The BMW M1 is a mid-engined sports car produced from 1978 to 1981, renowned for its racing heritage, Giugiaro design, and 3.5-liter inline-six engine. Overview and History The BMW M1 (model code E26) was BMW’s first mid-engine production car and the only one until the i8. It was developed by BMW Motorsport GmbH under Jochen Neerpasch to compete in sports car racing and to rival Porsche in Group 5 racing . Initially, BMW partnered with Lamborghini to produce the car, but due to financial and logistical issues, BMW took full control of production in 1978 A total of 453 units were built, including 399 road cars and 54 racing versions

BMW M SERIES

Like Dislike The BMW M series is a special lineup of high-performance cars manufactured by BMW M GmbH, a subsidiary of BMW AG. Originally created to support BMW's racing program, the M series vehicles are designed to deliver superior performance, agility, and power, making them the highest-performance versions of the BMW lineup . These cars are not just modified versions of standard BMW models; they are engineered for an exhilarating driving experience
